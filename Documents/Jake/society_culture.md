|----------|-----------|
| Earth | 4.1 billion |

Earth peaked at 11.2 billion in 2099. The decline is not a crisis — it was deliberate and welcomed. Lower population with longer lifespans means each individual life consumes more resources but contributes more over time. The math, as Jake's father wrote, "works out beautifully and no one in 2026 would have believed it."

## Language

Natural languages still exist and are celebrated as cultural heritage. However:

- All NI-equipped humans have real-time translation (since NI-4, 2085)
- Language barriers are a historical curiosity
- English, Mandarin, Spanish, Arabic remain most common native languages
- **Solang** — a creole that emerged organically in space communities — is Jake's native language alongside English and French

> "In 2026 I find myself translating everything twice — once from whatever language is spoken, once from whatever they mean by it. The second translation is harder." — Jake, personal log day 33

## Family and Relationships

With lifespans of 300+ years, traditional family structures collapsed and rebuilt multiple times. What emerged by 2326:

### Core Bonds
2–5 people in long-term "life partnerships" — not necessarily romantic, not necessarily permanent. The legal structure around partnership is flexible by design.

### Extended Networks
20–50 people in chosen family clusters. These are the primary social unit. Biological family remains important but is one node in a larger network, not the defining structure.

### Children
Rare and considered precious. Average person has 0.8 children across their entire life. Childhoods are longer by design — children are not expected to be "productive" until age 25. Parenting is an intense 25-year project that most people do once, some do twice.

### Long-term Relationships
"Century partners" — people who remain together 100+ years — are celebrated and studied. They are not common. Most relationships span decades, transform, and end amicably. The concept of "failed relationship" is considered an outdated framing — relationships end when they've given what they had to give.

Jake's parents are century partners (126 years together at Jake's departure). He considers this extraordinary.

## Work and Purpose

Universal Basic Existence (UBE) covers all survival needs since [[history_timeline#2069 — The Quiet Revolution|2069]]. "Work" in the 2026 sense barely exists.

People pursue **life chapters** — extended periods of focus on one domain:

| Chapter type | Description | Typical duration |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Contribution | Long-term creative/scientific projects | 40–80 years |
| Craft | Mastery for its own sake | 20–60 years |
| Care | Raising children, community | 25–40 years |
| Exploration | Physical and intellectual wandering | 10–30 years |
| Rest | Deliberate stillness | 2–10 years |

Most people cycle through 5–8 chapters across their lives. Jake's father spent 60 years as a historian, 20 years as a furniture maker, and is currently (at 247) in his third contribution chapter — writing his second major historical work.

> "In 2026 they ask each other 'what do you do?' meaning what job. In 2326 we ask 'what are you working on?' It sounds similar. It's completely different." — Jake, personal log day 19

## What Happened to Nations

Traditional nation-states faded between 2100–2200. Not through conflict — through irrelevance.

**Replaced by**:
- **Culture Zones** — regions defined by shared values, aesthetics, and lifestyle preferences, not ethnicity or borders
- **The Grand Council** — handles planetary decisions (climate, space, resource allocation, AI governance)
- Culture Zones handle local governance, cultural preservation, and internal matters

**Europe Culture Zone** (Jake's home): Known for historical consciousness, architectural preservation, academic culture. Includes former EU nations plus North Africa and Turkey.

**Nationalist movements**: Studied in history classes as a psychological phenomenon — specifically how scarcity and fear of the other interacted to produce identity-based tribalism. Considered to have "resolved itself" once UBE eliminated material scarcity.

## Religion and Philosophy

### Surviving Religions (2326 status)
- **Christianity**: ~800 million practitioners, heavily transformed; most denominations embraced NI technology
- **Islam**: ~1.2 billion, significant internal diversity between NI-accepting and Natural Human branches  
- **Buddhism**: ~600 million, often cited as the religion that "adapted best" to the Longevity Breakthrough
- **Hinduism**: ~700 million, significant philosophical renaissance around consciousness backup debates
- **New traditions**: Dozens of significant post-2100 spiritual movements

### Dominant Philosophy: Temporal Humanism
The belief that human experience across time is sacred, and that understanding the past is as important as building the future.

Key tenets:
1. Every moment of human experience has intrinsic value
2. The dead deserve to be remembered with accuracy, not nostalgia
3. Those who come after us will be shaped by what we build now
4. Time is the most precious resource — not money, not energy, not attention

This is partly why temporal research exists — understanding the past is considered a moral obligation.

**Related**: [[jake_personal]], [[technology#Time Research — Jake's Field]]

### The Signal Schools
Philosophical movements that emerged after [[history_timeline#2267 — The Kepler Anomaly|the 2267 Kepler Anomaly]] revealed that time is non-linear. Major branches:

- **Eternalists**: All moments exist simultaneously; free will is an illusion; everything that happens was always going to happen
- **Branchists**: The Kepler Anomaly created a branch; we exist in one of infinite timelines; Jake's visit to 2026 created a new branch
- **Continuists**: Time is one river; the anomaly was a ripple, not a split; history is self-consistent

Jake is privately a Continuist. He doesn't discuss it.

### Natural Human Territories
Communities that reject neural interfaces. Formed 2156, currently ~180 million people.

> "I spent two weeks in a Natural Human Territory before my mission. No NI, no augmented reality, no AI companions. I thought I would find it limiting. I found it clarifying. I understand why they choose it. I also couldn't stay." — Jake, personal log, pre-departure notes

## Education

### Children
- Neural interface at age 7 (standard)
- Formal education to age 25: focus on creativity, ethics, relationships, embodied experience
- Factual knowledge accessed via NI rather than memorized

### Adults
- Most people complete 3–6 formal education periods across their lives
- Universities are not primarily for young people
- The most prestigious seminars are often attended by people in their 100s and 200s
- Jake completed 2 degrees before his mission (age 91 at departure, mission at 16 in temporal displacement)

## Class and Inequality in 2326

Material inequality essentially eliminated. New inequalities emerged:

**Time inequality**: Access to rare human expertise — a 200-year master artisan's time is precious and not infinitely scalable.

**Attention inequality**: Some people have larger networks, more influence, more visibility. This is the primary status currency.

**Experience inequality**: Certain experiences are finite — Mars permits are limited, temporal research slots are extremely scarce.

**Social isolation**: Considered the primary form of poverty. Being outside meaningful community networks is the worst thing that can happen to a person in 2326.

> "In 2026 poverty is material — food, shelter, medical care. In 2326 poverty is relational — to be without meaningful connection is to be truly destitute. Both are solvable. Both require political will. 2026 is beginning to figure out the first. The second took until 2150." — Jake, personal log day 55
