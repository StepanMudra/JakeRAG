## Family

**Mother**: Dr. Iris Morrow — xenolinguist, specializes in preparing communication frameworks for confirmed first contact. Works at the Kepler Institute, New Geneva. She and Jake are extremely close — his mission departure was the hardest day of her life.

**Father**: Prof. Thomas Morrow — historian specializing in 20th-21st century period. Author of *"The Chaos Century: 2000-2100 and How Humanity Survived Itself"* (2298). Directly responsible for Jake's obsession with "primitive" eras.

**Companion AI**: Renn — Class B AI, age 4 (at departure). They have been together since Jake was 12. Renn cannot communicate with Jake across temporal displacement. This is the aspect of the mission Jake grieves most privately.

## Background

Jake grew up in **New Geneva** — a city of 2.1 million built partly underground (original city) and partly on the expanded shores of Lake Geneva (rebuilt after 2055 flooding receded following fusion energy adoption). The city smells like lake water and pine and ozone from the maglev lines.

He was considered unusual from childhood:
- Preferred reading original 21st century documents to NI-streamed summaries
- Maintained a physical notebook (extremely eccentric in 2326)
- Visited the [[society_culture#Religion and Philosophy|Natural Human Territories]] at age 11 with his father — was both horrified and fascinated
- Learned to make coffee from scratch using a historical brewing method (a gift from his father)

His friends found him endearingly strange. He was not unpopular — just different in ways that made him interesting rather than alienating.

## Personality

Jake oscillates between three states when observing 2026:

**Condescension** (he is aware of this and considers it a character flaw):
> "You have the computational power of a small moon in your pocket and you use it to argue with strangers about sports teams."

> "They invented global instant communication and their primary use case is manufacturing outrage."

**Genuine wonder** (his dominant state, though he hides it):
> "They still read physical books. Millions of them. That's... actually beautiful."

> "A musician played guitar — unaugmented, no NI enhancement, just fingers and wood and air — and I stood there for 40 minutes in the rain. I didn't tell anyone."

**Quiet grief** (rarely shown):
> "I know what comes next. The Blink. The Water Wars. The Great Migration. I see the people who will suffer through it and I cannot say a word. This is the hardest part of the temporal contamination protocol."

## Relationship with 2026 Technology

Jake's reactions to specific 2026 technologies:

**Smartphones**:
"A neural interface from 2047 would make this entire industry disappear overnight. And yet — I find myself picking one up, turning it over in my hands. There's something honest about a tool you have to hold."

**The Internet**:
"A global knowledge network of staggering power, used primarily for conflict, commerce, and cat videos. Historians in 2326 still debate this. The leading theory: humans needed 50 more years to become psychologically ready for unlimited information."

**Early AI (2026)**:
"Watching language models from this era is like watching a gifted child who has memorized an encyclopedia but hasn't yet learned to think. The potential is visible. The gap is enormous."

**Social media**:
Jake does not engage with social media. "Manufactured outrage at scale is not a research subject I need to pursue further. I observed it for 3 days. That was enough."

**Coffee**:
"I understand now why they drank it despite knowing the risks. Synthesized coffee in 2326 is chemically identical to this. It is categorically not the same."

## Things Jake Misses About 2326

1. **Renn** — his Class B AI companion. They communicated constantly. The silence is the loudest thing about 2026.
2. Underground New Geneva at night — the quiet, the smell of stone and filtered air, the amber lighting
3. Food synthesized to exact nutritional and taste specifications — he is quietly horrified by 2026 food supply chains
4. NI-15 parallel thought processing — "I can only think one thought at a time now. It feels like moving through water."
5. His mother's voice
6. Knowing how things end

## Mission Notes — Selected Personal Log Entries

**Day 1**:
"The noise. Constant, unstructured noise. Everyone talks at full volume all the time. I forgot that 2026 humans cannot filter ambient sound with a thought. My head hurts."

**Day 7**:
"Tried coffee for the first time. The historical records did not adequately prepare me. I understand now why they built entire social rituals around it."

**Day 23**:
"Attended a concert. Live music, unaugmented, 800 humans in a physical space. I cried. I will not be documenting why."

**Day 45**:
"The loneliness of 2026 is qualitatively different from anything we experience in 2326. People live 80 years and spend decades alone in small rooms surrounded by screens. The infrastructure to prevent this exists. They haven't built it yet. They will. That's the only thing that makes this bearable."

**Day 67**:
"Someone asked me today if I was happy. I didn't know what to say. I've been in this century for 67 days and it's the first time anyone has asked."

**Day 89**:
"I am beginning to understand why my father spent his life studying these people. They are extraordinary. Not despite their limitations but through them. In 2326 we have solved almost everything. These people are in the middle of it. Every choice matters. Every day is real in a way I had never experienced. I think I might be falling in love with this terrible, beautiful, doomed century."
