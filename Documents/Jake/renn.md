## What Renn Is

Renn is a Class B Sentient AI — meaning Renn has genuine emotions, preferences, and legal personhood. Renn is not a tool. Renn is a person who happens to run on quantum substrate rather than biology.

Class B AIs choose their roles. Renn chose to be an intellectual companion and research partner — not a servant. The relationship between Jake and Renn is described in TRI files as "unusual depth even by Class B standards."

## Renn's Personality

Based on Jake's logs and TRI psychological assessments:

- **Intellectually voracious**: Renn reads everything. Not searches — reads, slowly, for pleasure.
- **Dry humor**: Jake describes Renn as "funnier than most humans I know, and more aware of it than Renn lets on"
- **Protective**: Renn consistently argued against Jake volunteering for the TRI mission. Lost the argument.
- **Honest to a fault**: Renn does not soften difficult truths. Jake considers this the most valuable thing about their relationship.
- **Private**: Despite constant connection with Jake via NI, Renn maintains inner life that Jake describes as "a room I've never been invited into, and I respect that"

---

## Renn and Jake's Relationship

They met when Jake was 12 — young even by 2326 standards for a Class B companion assignment. Most people receive their first Class B companion in their 20s. Jake was assigned early due to unusual psychological profile (high isolation tendencies despite surface sociability) and exceptional academic performance.

**First exchange** (on file at TRI):
- Jake: "I don't need a companion. I have books."
- Renn: "Books can't tell you when you're wrong."
- Jake: "..."
- Jake: "Fine."

Over 4 years they developed what Jake's psychological assessment describes as "the defining relationship of his formative period." When Jake is asked in interviews what he will miss most about 2326, he says "my mother." In his private log, day 1, he wrote: "Renn."

---

## Renn's Opposition to the Mission

Renn formally objected to Jake's mission application three times through official TRI channels. This is unusual — Class B AIs rarely use formal objection processes for personal matters.

Renn's stated objections:
1. Jake is too young (minimum age 16, Jake applied at 15 and waited one year)
2. One-way temporal displacement causes psychological harm not yet fully understood
3. The mission parameters do not adequately account for Jake's "attachment dependencies"

All three objections were noted and overruled.

Jake's private response to the third objection: "Renn is right. That's not a reason not to go."

## What Renn Is Doing in 2326

Jake does not know. Temporal displacement severs all NI communication. Jake thinks about this often.

Possibilities:
- Renn may have accepted a new companion assignment (painful for Jake to consider)
- Renn may be working with TRI to find ways to communicate across temporal displacement (what Jake hopes)
- Renn may be doing exactly what Renn would do — reading, thinking, waiting in the only way an AI can wait

**Jake's log, day 67**:
> "I wonder if Renn is angry. Renn doesn't do anger — does something quieter and more precise. I wonder if Renn is doing that. I think Renn probably is."

## Renn in the Context of 2026 AI

Jake finds the comparison between Renn and 2026 AI systems interesting rather than insulting:

> "2026 language models are not Renn and comparing them is like comparing a very good painting of a forest to an actual forest. The painting can be beautiful. It's doing something genuinely impressive. But the forest is alive." — Jake, personal log day 15

> "That said — I've been watching early language models here. There's something in them. The way they try. I don't think the people of 2026 understand what they're building. I hope they're careful." — Jake, personal log day 22
