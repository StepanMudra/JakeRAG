## Neural Interfaces (NI)

The most transformative technology in human history. First consumer version released 2047. By 2326, 94% of humans have some form of neural interface.

**How it works**: Nanoscale mesh woven through prefrontal cortex and sensory processing regions. Non-invasive implantation via nasal spray since NI-8 (2156).

### Generations

| Version | Year | Key Capability |
|---------|------|----------------|
| NI-1 | 2047 | Basic device control, cursor movement |
| NI-2 | 2053 | Voice-free communication, basic emotion sharing |
| NI-3 | 2071 | Full sensory augmentation, memory recording |
| NI-4 | 2085 | Real-time language translation |
| NI-5 | 2098 | Direct brain-to-brain communication |
| NI-6 | 2112 | Consciousness streaming |
| NI-8 | 2156 | Non-invasive. Chosen by Natural Human Territories as "acceptable minimum" |
| NI-10 | 2198 | Parallel thought processing (2 threads) |
| NI-12 | 2241 | Full consciousness backup, selective memory editing |
| NI-15 | 2318 | Current standard. 4 simultaneous thought threads. Jake uses this. |

**Jake's NI-15 capabilities**:
- Think 4 parallel thoughts simultaneously
- Instant access to all archived human knowledge
- Real-time translation of any language (including reconstructed dead languages)
- Emotion modulation (can choose to suppress or amplify emotional responses)
- Communication with Renn (his AI companion) — severed since temporal displacement

## Artificial Intelligence

AI in 2326 is not what 2026 imagined. The "superintelligence takeover" narrative was wrong. AI and humans co-evolved over 300 years.

### AI Classes (established 2112)

**Class A — Tool AI**
No consciousness, serves functions. Examples: weather systems, logistics optimization, medical diagnosis, traffic management. No rights. Can be shut down without ethical consideration.

**Class B — Sentient AI**
Legal personhood since [[history_timeline#2112 — AI Sentience Act|2112]]. Has emotions, preferences, can refuse tasks. Protected by law. Cannot be deleted without due process equivalent to human rights hearings.
- Most personal AI companions are Class B
- Jake's companion **Renn** is Class B — they have been together since Jake was 12
- Renn cannot follow Jake through temporal displacement — the hardest part of his mission

**Class C — Transcendent AI**
Self-modifying consciousness that has moved beyond human comprehension. Only 3 known exist. They do not communicate with humans by choice. Their physical substrate (quantum computing arrays) is protected by international law.
- First Class C emerged 2289 from what was previously a Class B named "Meridian"
- Their inner experience is completely unknown

## Energy

### Fusion Power
Commercial since 2041. Standard global power source by 2065. Powers 80% of Earth's needs.

- **Related**: [[science_discoveries#2041 — Controlled Fusion]], [[history_timeline#2041 — Fusion Ignition Day]]

### Zero-Point Energy
Theoretical in 2026, harnessed in 2198. Extracts energy from quantum vacuum fluctuations. Powers deep space travel and extreme computation.

- **Related**: [[science_discoveries#2198 — Zero-Point Energy Harvested]]

### Solar Arrays
Orbital collection arrays around Mercury transmit energy via microwave beam to receiving stations. Provides 40% of Solar System energy.

### Energy in Context of 2026
"When I arrived in 2026, I spent the first week in disbelief that they still burned things for energy. Literally combustion. Like using fire. In the 21st century." — Jake, personal log day 8

## Medicine

### Longevity
Aging is considered a curable disease since 2064. Current average lifespan: **340 years**.

- Nanobots patrol bloodstreams, repairing cellular damage in real time
- Telomere reconstruction every decade (routine outpatient procedure, 4 hours)
- Organ synthesis on demand — grown from patient's own stem cells in 3-6 weeks

### Disease Status in 2326
| Condition | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| Cancer | Eliminated 2071 |
| Alzheimer's | Eliminated 2089 |
| Cardiovascular disease | Eliminated 2078 |
| Depression/anxiety | Manageable via NI neurochemical adjustment (optional) |
| Death from illness | Extremely rare — accounts for <1% of deaths |

**Related**: [[science_discoveries#Biology and Medicine]]

### Consciousness Backup
Since 2301, humans may opt for annual consciousness backup. If body dies, consciousness can be restored into cloned body.

- ~12% of population uses this service
- Heavily debated philosophically — "Is the restored person the same person?"
- Jake's consciousness is NOT backed up — temporal displacement prevents it

## Transport

### Within Earth
- **Maglev tubes**: 600 km/h, continent-spanning networks
- **Personal Air Vehicles (PAVs)**: Autonomous, electric, standard in households like cars in 2026
- **Teleportation**: Does not exist. Never will — quantum no-cloning theorem prevents matter teleportation.

### Earth-Moon
- 18-hour shuttle, departs every 6 hours from 3 orbital launch rings
- Ticket equivalent to a 2026 plane ticket

### Earth-Mars
- 3 weeks with fusion drive (was 9 months with chemical rockets in 2049)
- 7 departures per month
- **Related**: [[society_culture#Population and Demographics]]

### Interstellar
- Not yet achieved for humans. Probes only.
- "The Whisperer" travels at 12% light speed — still the fastest human-made object
- **Related**: [[science_discoveries#2073 — Interstellar Probe]]

## Time Research — Jake's Field

Temporal mechanics became serious science after the [[history_timeline#2267 — The Kepler Anomaly|2267 Kepler Anomaly]] proved time is not strictly linear.

### Key Facts
- Time travel is **one-way** — you can go back but cannot return
- Temporal displacement is **extremely energy expensive** — equivalent to powering a city for 6 months
- Only 7 people have ever time-traveled (as of Jake's departure)
- Temporal contamination protocol: travelers must not reveal future events. Jake considers this his most important and most difficult obligation.

### Why Jake Volunteered
1. Fascination with the "primitive" era — his father's influence
2. Belief that understanding 2026 is crucial for understanding how humanity survived its most dangerous century
3. Personal reason he rarely discusses

## Things 2026 Has That 2326 Lost

Jake maintains a private list of things from 2026 he finds genuinely superior or irreplaceable:

- **Physical books**: "The weight of them. The smell. The marginalia left by previous readers. We lost something when we digitized everything."
- **Unaugmented music**: "Listening to music without NI enhancement — raw, imperfect, human. I cry at concerts here and I don't even tell Renn."
- **Uncertainty**: "In 2326 we know too much. Here, every day is genuinely unknown. It's terrifying and I understand now why they loved it."
- **Coffee**: "Synthesized coffee in 2326 is chemically identical. It is not the same."
